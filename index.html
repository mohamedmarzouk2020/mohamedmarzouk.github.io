About Me

I am got my Ph.D. in computational chemistry from School of Life Sciences and Technology , Tokyo Institute of Technology .

I am currently a postdoctoral researcher in Molecular Modeling and Simulation (MMS) Team, National Institute for Quantum Science and Technology (QST), Chiba, Japan.

I am working on a project to quantitatively express protein stability in order to predict the effects of mutations (especially addition/deletion mutations) on protein stability via alchemical transformation using one of the enhanced sampling techniques called replica exchange with solute tempering (REST) in combination with free-energy perturbation (FEP).

In addition, to further explore the biological slow processes (in particular binding/unbinding (for protein−DNA, and protein−ligand), and open/close of binding pockets), I am combining the enhanced sampling approach of parallel cascade selection molecular dynamics (PaCS-MD) with the Markov state model (MSM).
